In the Tasman Sea, the name of this surf break speaks for itself. At Shipstern Bluff, the chances of getting out of there alive on a big day are lower than the chances of riding wipeout-free monsters. But what makes this surf break so dangerous and scary is definitely its famous "step."

Shipstern Bluff is a mutant wave that creates a wave within a wave due to the shape of the reef bottom. When steps emerge, surfers are forced to handle the jumps as a heavy and fast-breaking lip falls over. Tasmania's most fearful surf spot can only be accessed by either a 30-kilometer jet ski or a boat ride or a two-hour hike through the Tasman National Park.
